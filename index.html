"Moron" Genes - Not an Insult, but Named Correctly

  • Definition: Extra genes inserted by bacteriophages into a host genome, unrelated to phage replication, often adding new traits.
  • Name origin: In genetic parlance, a "moron" is more DNA than the phage needs for its own life cycle.
  • Can encode toxins, adhesion factors, or metabolic enzymes ‚Äî flipping a harmless bacterium into a pathogen.
  • Chemistry view: a bonus catalytic module added to an existing reaction network, altering outputs drastically.

Term popularized in phage genomics; see Hendrix et al., 2000.
